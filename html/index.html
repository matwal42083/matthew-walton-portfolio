Matthew Walton intro bio

I'm Matthew Walton, a Full-Stack Developer based in Missouri. I spent my formative years in Mount Vernon, Washington, during the 1980s and 90s. My grandfather, a passionate computer enthusiast, instilled in me a deep curiosity for technology. He constantly encouraged me to explore the digital world, predicting that handheld computers would one day be ubiquitous. At the time, I dismissed his notion as outlandish. It wasn't until 2006 or 2007, years after his passing, that the first iPhone commercial validated his foresight. My early experiences were primarily with Windows PCs due to their accessibility. However, I've always preferred other operating systems. I find the Windows ecosystem unnecessarily complex and commercially driven. Apple products, with their proprietary approach, are equally unappealing. I recall Apple's marketing campaign in the early 2000s attempting to differentiate Macs from PCs, despite Steve Jobs himself coining the term "personal computer" for Apple machines in earlier decades. While I appreciate Linux, my extensive history with Windows led me to adopt a hybrid approach, utilizing WSL with Linux Ubuntu in my terminal. For gaming,

I prefer dedicated consoles like the PS4 or PS5. I also avoid third-party antivirus software, finding it often redundant and resource-intensive, given the built-in security features of Windows and Google Chrome. I've witnessed firsthand the evolution of the digital landscape, from the pre-internet era to the advent of residential internet access, AOL dial-up, Netscape Navigator, and the rise of email. Unbeknownst to my teacher, I was already familiar with electronic communication through my avid use of Napster, a revolutionary platform for music sharing that predates even Spotify. For over three years, I've honed my skills as a Full-Stack Developer, contributing to various projects. I'm driven by a desire to share my knowledge and empower others in the realm of technology and coding. A strong advocate for open-source software, I'm eager to continue learning and expanding my expertise. I'm actively seeking a role that will grant me the independence to further pursue my passion for software development, a pursuit I currently maintain outside of my regular work hours.
