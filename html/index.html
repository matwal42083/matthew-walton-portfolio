One day my grandpa told me that I needed to learn a lot about computers and to become an IT professional because he told me when I grew up that, everybody would have a computer and everyone would be carrying a handheld computer. I looked at him like he was crazy. I stated “Why”, he stated “You just don’t understand, You're too young to even comprehend this. But this will be important to you, for everything. I was a teenager when the internet came out for the first time. I was excited about it. These were the days of the Netscape. Which was an internet browser and service provider as well. Then AOL came out, It was a service provider and a browser as well. I was a big user of Napster, the shared music files system. Back then, guys like me looked up to Sean Parker and Sean Fanning. Two 19-year-old childhood friends who grew up together, and later on built Napster. Napster was very impressive back then. There was nothing like it. It was a shared music file system, which means that your sharing music with other people. It starts with someone uploading files to the system, then someone else searches for the music then downloads it. Then more people upload more music files with a wider range. Every time you search for music, it looks for the closest computer that has it, and then it returns a list of all the closest to farthest of that one particular song, artist, or album depending on what you searched. Each item on the list has the song title, artist name, duration of the song, album title, the location of the computer it’s on, how long to download it, then a progress bar for download progress, then there was a way to tell if the song or whatever it was, if its safe, secure and not a virus. The bad part about Napster is that you had to be careful, very careful. Because you're actually at risk of accidentally downloading a virus. That was the first thing you had to check for, if it was safe and secure, one wrong move and it was a bad day for anybody that didn’t pay attention.
